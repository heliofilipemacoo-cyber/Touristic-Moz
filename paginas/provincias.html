Introduction & First Considerations

Mozambique is divided into 10 provinces plus Maputo City, which holds provincial status of its own. To keep this guide genuinely useful for travelers rather than an exhaustive administrative list, we focus only on the provinces that offer real value for a typical trip: places with established tourist infrastructure, notable attractions, and reasonably easy access.

Below you will find in-depth coverage of Maputo (capital and gateway to the country), Inhambane (beaches and diving), Nampula (home to the UNESCO-listed Ilha de Moçambique), Sofala (Gorongosa National Park and wildlife), and Cabo Delgado (the Quirimbas Archipelago).

The remaining provinces — Niassa, Zambézia, Tete, Manica, and Gaza — are not covered in detail here. This isn't because they lack value; it's simply that they see far fewer international visitors, have less tourist infrastructure in place, and are generally harder to reach without a longer, more independent trip. If your travel style leans toward off-the-beaten-path exploration, they're worth researching separately.

Maputo

Mozambique's capital is where most trips begin — a lively mix of jacaranda-lined avenues, colonial-era architecture, and a fast-growing skyline overlooking the Baía de Maputo.

  • Best time: May–October
  • Getting there: International airport
  • Budget: $$–$$$
  • Malaria risk: Low

Other Attractions

  • Praça da Independência

    The city's central square, home to the City Hall and the statue of Samora Machel.

  • Photo coming soon
    CFM Railway Station

    A striking Beaux-Arts building often linked to Gustave Eiffel's studio, still Maputo's working train station.

A Bit of History

Founded as a Portuguese trading post and long known as Lourenço Marques, the city was renamed Maputo after independence in 1975. Its historic centre still shows a strong Portuguese colonial influence, blended with Art Deco buildings from the early 20th century.

Culture

Maputo has a vibrant arts and music scene — marrabenta music has its roots here — along with a lively café and nightlife culture along Avenida Julius Nyerere, and craft markets like FEIMA selling local art and design.

Map of Maputo - Google Maps

Why go: Maputo is the easiest entry point to Mozambique, with the country's main international airport, the best infrastructure, and a good first taste of the food and culture before heading further out.

Inhambane

Mozambique's most popular beach destination, known above all for Tofo Beach — one of the few places in the world with a near year-round chance of swimming with whale sharks and manta rays.

  • Best time: June–October (whale sharks)
  • Getting there: ~1h flight from Maputo
  • Budget: $$
  • Malaria risk: Moderate

Other Attractions

  • Photo coming soon
    Barra Beach

    Calmer waters, popular for families and snorkeling near the reef.

  • Photo coming soon
    Inhambane City

    A charming, low-key town with some of the oldest colonial buildings in the country.

A Bit of History

Inhambane is one of the oldest continuously inhabited settlements on the East African coast, with a trading history stretching back over a thousand years through Swahili-Arab and later Portuguese traders — making it one of the first parts of Mozambique to have contact with Portugal, well before Maputo grew into a city.

Culture

The region has a strong dhow-sailing tradition and a relaxed, beach-town pace of life. Cashew and coconut plantations shape both the landscape and the local cuisine.

Map of Inhambane - Google Maps

Why go: if beaches and marine life are the priority, Inhambane is the single best-value stop in the country — good infrastructure, direct flights, and world-class diving without needing the logistics of a more remote trip.

Sofala

Home to Gorongosa National Park, one of Africa's great wildlife recovery stories — a war-ravaged reserve rebuilt over the last two decades into a thriving safari destination.

  • Best time: May–November (dry season)
  • Getting there: ~3-4h drive from Beira
  • Budget: $$$
  • Malaria risk: High

Other Attractions

  • Rhino Sightings

    Part of the park's ongoing reintroduction program.

  • Beira

    Sofala's coastal capital, with Indian Ocean beaches and colonial architecture.

A Bit of History

Gorongosa was once one of Africa's premier safari parks in the 1960s-70s, before Mozambique's civil war (1977-1992) devastated its wildlife populations. A long-term restoration project since 2004 has brought numbers back dramatically, making it one of the continent's most remarkable conservation comeback stories.

Culture

The park works closely with surrounding communities on conservation and eco-tourism programs, and the wider Sofala region blends Chuabo and Sena cultural traditions with Beira's Portuguese-colonial port history.

Map of Sofala - Google Maps

Why go: for genuine safari wildlife in Mozambique, Gorongosa is the clearest choice — a strong alternative to Kruger or the Serengeti, with the added story of a park actively being rebuilt.

Nampula

Home to Ilha de Moçambique, a tiny coral island and UNESCO World Heritage Site that gave the entire country its name — packed with over 500 years of layered history.

  • Best time: April–November
  • Getting there: Flight to Nampula + short drive
  • Budget: $$
  • Malaria risk: High

Other Attractions

  • Photo coming soon
    Fortaleza de São Sebastião

    The oldest complete fort still standing in sub-Saharan Africa.

  • Photo coming soon
    Stone Town

    The island's historic quarter, with Swahili, Arab, Indian and Portuguese architecture side by side.

A Bit of History

Ilha de Moçambique was a major Indian Ocean trading post centuries before Portuguese arrival, and later became the capital of Portuguese East Africa until 1898. Its layered Swahili-Arab-Indian-Portuguese history is why it was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1991.

Culture

The island blends Makhuwa, Swahili and Portuguese influences, visible in its architecture, cuisine, and the capulana cloth traditions still practiced by local artisans today.

Map of Nampula - Google Maps

Why go: for travelers who want history and culture over beaches or safari, Ilha de Moçambique offers something no other province does — an entire town frozen in centuries of trade history, still lived in today.

Cabo Delgado

⚠ Safety Notice: Cabo Delgado is currently subject to "avoid all travel" advisories from several governments (including the US, UK, Canada and Australia) due to an active insurgency in the province since 2017. Before planning any visit, check your own government's official travel advisory for Mozambique.

The Quirimbas Archipelago

Historically, Cabo Delgado has been home to the Quirimbas Archipelago — a chain of pristine coral islands considered one of Mozambique's most exceptional destinations. Stretching for over 400 km along the coast, it's made up of around 32 coral islands, most of them uninhabited and untouched by mass tourism.

  • Malaria risk: High

Other Attractions

    Ibo Island

  • Photo coming soon
    Pemba & Wimbi Beach

    The provincial capital, sitting on one of the largest natural bays in the world, with a relaxed, palm-lined coastline.

A Bit of History

Long before European colonization, Cabo Delgado's coast — particularly Ibo Island — was a significant Swahili-Arab trading post, connecting East Africa to the Indian Ocean trade routes. In more recent years, the discovery of major offshore natural gas reserves has drawn significant international investment to the province, reshaping its economy alongside the security challenges mentioned above.

Culture

The province is home to the Makonde people, celebrated across East Africa for their intricate wood carving and the traditional Mapiko mask dance, as well as the Mwani, a coastal community with strong Swahili-Arab trading roots dating back centuries. Inland, the province borders the Rovuma River, the natural frontier with Tanzania.

Current outlook: given the ongoing security situation, we're not able to recommend independent travel to this province at this time. If you have a specific reason to travel there, consult a specialized security advisor and your embassy beforehand.
